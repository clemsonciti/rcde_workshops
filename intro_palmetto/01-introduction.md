# Introduction

Palmetto 2 is a *supercomputing cluster*: a set of powerful computers that are connected to each other. It is built and maintained by Clemson University, and is located in an off-campus facility close to Anderson SC. The Palmetto 2 cluster has a dedicated power plant. 

:::{figure} ../fig/intro_palmetto/01-palmetto-front-view.png
A view of Palmetto Cluster
:::

The Palmetto 2 cluster is maintained by two teams: a team of *system administrators*, who work directly on the cluster, and monitor its hardware and operating system, and a team of *research facilitators*, who work with Palmetto 2 users. The two teams work very closely together. As research facilitators, we provide training workshops, online help and support, and office hours through Microsoft Appointments.


We maintain a [very extensive website](https://www.palmetto.clemson.edu/palmetto/) which hosts plenty of information for Palmetto 2 users. We have more than 2000 people using Palmetto 2. Users come from a variety of departments: Computer Science, Chemistry, Biology, Public Health, Engineering, Mathematics, Psychology, Forestry, etc. In addition to researchers at Clemson, we have researchers from many other universities using Palmetto 2.
